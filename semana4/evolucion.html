Birds are a group of warm-blooded, feathered creatures with wings and a beak or bill. The evolution of birds is believed to have occurred over millions of years, starting with feathered dinosaurs. During the Jurassic period, birds evolved from small, fast-running dinosaurs. Over time, they developed wing structures and feathers that allowed them to fly. Today, birds have adapted to different environments, with adaptations such as powerful beaks for catching prey, specialized feet for climbing trees, and streamlined bodies for long-distance migration. The evolution of birds continues to be studied and understood by scientists.
